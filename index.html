About the Human Eye

The human eye is an important sense organ. It receives light, focuses it, and sends signals to the brain. The brain understands these signals and forms the image we see.

Main Parts of the Eye

Cornea

Protects the eye and helps light enter.

Iris

Controls the size of the pupil.

Pupil

Allows light to enter the eye.

Lens

Focuses light on the retina.

Retina

Converts light into nerve signals.

Optic Nerve

Sends signals from the eye to the brain.
